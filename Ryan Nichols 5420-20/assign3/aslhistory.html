History

The first sign language got its start in the mid 18th century in France by a man named Abbe de l'Epee who was a cleric. He witnessed two deaf girls using hand signs to communicate to each other. From this experience he envisioned a school where deaf children could be educated through a signed language. In 1771 he founded the first school for the deaf in France.
The largest influencer of sign in America was Thomas Hopkins Gallaudet. He traveled to France to work with l'Epee, and together they modified the French sign to become what is known as ASL today. Just like any language it has gone through many transformations, but these to iconic figures are known for their development and contributions to modern day ASL.

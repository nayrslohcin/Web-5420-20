What is ASL

ASL or American Sign Language is the official language used by the deaf community in the United States, and English speaking areas of Canada.
ASL is the language of a sizeable minority. Estimates range from 500,000 to two million speakers in the U.S. alone; there are also many speakers in Canada. Compared to data from the Census Bureau, which counts other language minorities, ASL is the leading minority language in the U.S. after the "big four": Spanish, Italian, German, and French (http://libguides.gallaudet.edu/).

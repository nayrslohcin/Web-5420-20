Expression

Facial expressions, posture, and movement are critical componants to ASL. Signs used with different expressions are important when conveying your message to others. It could mean the difference between the other person thinking you are angry with them, or simply asking a question.
One example of faciel expressions combined with the language is that of "WH" words. Like what, who, where, and why. When using these words in a question that requires more then a "yes/no" answer it is important that you lower your eyebrows.
This type of an experssion is called a non-manual marker. Following is a list of non-manual markers used in ASL.
  • Head nods
  • Raised eyebrows
  • Tilted head
  • Pursed lips
  • Eye shifts
  • Eye gazes
  • Facial expressions (smile, anger, frown, puzzled look, etc)
  • Body shifts / movements

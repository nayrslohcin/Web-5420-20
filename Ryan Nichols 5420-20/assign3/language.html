ASL as a Language

ASL is a full featured language with sentence structure, and grammar. It is a common mistake among hearing people that ASL is English in sign, this is not true. There is a dialect of sign called Signed English or SE. This sign uses the same sentence structure, and grammar as English. ASL on the other hand is as different from English as Japanese is in regard to sentence structure, and grammar.
When a deaf person who speaks ASL writes a sentence down on paper, native English speakers recognize the words, but becuase the structure does not flow like English they believe that ASL signers only know broken english, when infact the ASL signer is writing in ASL.
ASL also has roots in French. Even though they two languages are quite different now, there are some signed words in ASL that are representative of their French roots. (http://www.gallaudet.edu/dpn-home/sign-language.html)

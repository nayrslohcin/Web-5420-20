Gene Kelly

Gene Kelly was born August 23, 1912 in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. He brought an athletic style to tap dance, and dance in general. In his younger years he tought dance lessons in college, to help pay for his education. He starred in several Broadway productions such as Leave it to Me, One For the Money, and Pal Joey. He soon went to Hollywood and starred in and directed many films. Some of the films he played in are For Me and My Gal, Anchors Aweigh, An American in Paris, and Singing in the Rain.

He explained his dance style as "All of my dancing came out of the idea of the common man." He brought his own style and popularity to art of tap dance.

Kelly died on February 2, 1996

Gregory Hines

Gregory Hines was born February 14, 1946 in New York, New York. He started learning tap early in his life. He was part of the group Hines, Hines, and Dad, which included his father and older brothers. He studied tap under Henry Le Tang and danced much of his early career at the Apollow Theater.

In 1992 he started his broadway career where he earned a Tony Award for his role in Jelly's Last Jam. He also started in several films, and is best known for his roles in The Cotton Club and White Nights.

Gregory died of cancer August 9, 2003 at the age of 57.

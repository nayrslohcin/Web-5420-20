Savion Glover

Savion Glover was born on November 19, 1973 in Nework, New Jersey. He started learning rythm as a percusionist, but soon left to go to the Newark Community School of the Arts, where he recieved a full scholarship. He started taking tap at age seven and quickly took to rhythm tap. He is well known for his own style of tap called 'hitting.'

Savion has stared in several broadway musicals such as The Tap Dance Kid, Black and Blue, and Jelly Roll. He coreographed Bring in 'Da Noise, Bring in 'Da Funk, which won four Tony Awards in 1996. This also brought him the Tony award for best choreographer.

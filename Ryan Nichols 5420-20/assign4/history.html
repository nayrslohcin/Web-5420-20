Modern tap dance is a combination of early African Americans combining their footwork with Irish and British clogging steps. Modern tap dance has been associated with four early dancers: William Henry Lane, George H. Primrose, King Rastus Brown, and Bill "Bojangles" Robinson. Although tap has roots that go back to the 1800's, the term tap dance did not appear in print until around 1928. The modern tap dance shoe with metal heal and toe taps appeared in about 1910.

In modern times we have such names as Gene Kelly, Sammy Davis Jr., Gregory Hines, and Savion Glover. Tap dance has been a part of American culture for 300 years. It's combination of its Irish and African roots prove that it is truly an American tradition. Whether you enjoy wathing, or partaking in it it has a mesmerizing affect on those who experience it. For an indepth look at its diverse and rich history go to: http://memory.loc.gov/diglib/ihas/loc.natlib.ihas.200217630/default.html.
